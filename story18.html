Teboho’s Journey: From Exam Failure to Software Developer and Homeowner

After failing his final high school exams at Hill Rock High School, Teboho felt the weight of disappointment and shame. But instead of giving up, he turned his pain into purpose. With no money for college, he discovered Udemy — an online platform offering affordable coding courses.

Teboho studied day and night using a borrowed phone and downloaded PDFs. He learned web development, mobile apps, and databases. His dedication was unmatched, and soon he began freelancing online, building websites for clients in Lesotho and abroad.

As his income grew, he registered his own tech company and started hiring other self-taught youth from villages like Serutle and Manamela. His reputation spread as a trusted developer delivering quality systems and digital solutions.

Today, Teboho owns multiple cars and lives in his own modern house in Berea. He also mentors students who didn’t pass high school, showing them that education is not limited to the classroom.

His story is proof that failure isn’t the end — it’s a new beginning if you’re willing to learn, grow, and push beyond your limits.
