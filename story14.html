Nthabiseng’s Journey: From Garden Harvests to Fruit & Veg Shop Owner

After completing high school, Nthabiseng returned home uncertain about her future. Her mother’s garden in Manamela had an abundance of cabbages and carrots, so she decided to start selling the fresh produce in her village. She worked tirelessly, walking long distances to deliver vegetables to customers and markets.

As her customer base grew, Nthabiseng’s small business began to outgrow the family garden. A kind neighbor in Serutle offered her a piece of land to farm. She expanded her crops, learning better planting methods and diversifying her produce to meet rising demand.

Her hard work paid off. With consistent profits and experience, Nthabiseng relocated to Maseru to pursue bigger opportunities. She opened a bustling Fruit & Veg shop, creating a reliable supply chain by purchasing fresh stock both from local farmers and South African farms.

Today, Nthabiseng is known as a young agripreneur who connects farmers with urban markets. Her shop not only supports her family but also boosts local farming communities, proving that small beginnings can grow into thriving enterprises.
