Tumelo’s Journey: From Contract Laborer to Rich Taxi Owner

At the age of 20, Tumelo from Serutle made a bold move toward financial independence. After working a tough one-year contract job in Sekubu, he was paid with a cow. Tumelo sold the cow for M8,000 and used the money to pursue his dream of farming.

He invested in potato seeds and rented land in Manamela. To enrich his soil, he used manure from his father’s animals. His first harvest was a success, and he quickly became a recognized young farmer supplying local markets with quality produce.

But Tumelo didn’t stop there. With savings from farming, he bought his first taxi and began working routes between villages and towns. His reliability and business sense allowed him to grow quickly in the transport industry.

Today, Tumelo is a proud taxi fleet owner in Leribe, with several taxis operating under his name. His income has grown significantly, and he is known as a successful and wealthy businessman in the region.

From a cow earned through hard work to a taxi empire—Tumelo's story teaches us that vision, sacrifice, and determination can change a person’s destiny.
