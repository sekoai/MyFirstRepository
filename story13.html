Mosuoe’s Journey: From Employee to Shoe Store Owner

Mosuoe grew up in Manamela village with dreams of building a better life. After high school, he found work in a Chinese shop in town, earning only M900 per month. Despite the low pay, Mosuoe was determined to change his life. He lived frugally, saving a portion of every salary with a clear goal in mind.

After months of discipline, Mosuoe used his savings to buy a few pairs of shoes and began selling them to friends and people in nearby villages. His dedication and honesty won the trust of his customers. Word spread quickly, and orders began to grow beyond what he could carry in his backpack.

Recognizing the demand, Mosuoe reinvested all profits back into the business. He expanded his stock, rented a small stall in Maseru, and soon his little shoe-selling hustle turned into a thriving shoe store.

Years of consistency paid off—Mosuoe now owns multiple shoe stores across Maseru. With steady profits, he bought his first delivery van, followed by more business cars to transport shoes across the city.

Mosuoe’s story inspires many in his village: With hard work, wise investment, and perseverance, dreams can become reality.
