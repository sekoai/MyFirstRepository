From Goats to Global Export

How 10 goats per year built an international wool empire

The Shepherd with a Vision

At age 20, Teboho took over his family's goat herd in Manamela village after his father's passing. While neighbors sold animals during droughts, he saw untapped potential. "Our mohair goats produced world-class wool, but we only got village prices," he recalls. "I vowed to change that."

Those first 10 goats were my startup capital. While others saw meat, I saw luxury fibers that could grace Parisian fashion houses. It just took the right connections and quality control.

The Wool Revolution Timeline

Global Reach

  • Launched Basotho Wool brand
