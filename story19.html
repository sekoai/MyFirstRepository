Karabo’s Journey: From Wood Scraps to Furniture Business Owner

In Serutle village, Karabo grew up helping his uncle with small carpentry jobs. After high school, with no money for college, he began collecting wood offcuts from construction sites and started making stools and small tables.

Using free online tutorials on YouTube, Karabo mastered furniture design, painting, and even marketing. He created a Facebook page and began receiving orders from people in Manamela, Maputsoe, and even Maseru.

His creativity, honesty, and work ethic helped him build a name. With his profits, he bought better tools and now runs a furniture workshop that supplies custom beds, wardrobes, and school desks.

Karabo employs five youth from his village and encourages them to use online learning like he did. He often says, “The internet is our new classroom, but only the hungry learn.”

His success shows youth that skills and online learning can create real income, right from the village.
