Ntsoaki’s Journey: From Backyard Beauty Hustle to Employer of 20 Women

After finishing high school in Manamela, Ntsoaki had no money to attend college, but she had a vision — to make people feel beautiful. With just a few hair products, nail polish, and a borrowed chair under a tree, she began her nails and hair beauty business.

She practiced day and night, watching videos, testing styles on her sisters and friends. Slowly, word of her skills spread around Manamela and nearby Serutle. With every customer, she reinvested into her growing dream.

Within a few years, Ntsoaki moved into a small rental space, bought salon chairs and modern tools, and added new services. Her professional service, creative hairstyles, and stunning nail designs brought her loyal clients from across districts.

Today, Ntsoaki owns a flourishing beauty studio and has hired 20 women — young ladies from villages like Manamela and Serutle — offering them skills, income, and a future.

Her story proves that with talent, consistency, and belief in yourself, you can build a business from scratch — and lift others as you rise.
