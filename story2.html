From Herding to Farming Empire

How cattle contracts built an agricultural business

Starting with Livestock Payments

At age 14, Mpho began working for Old Man Thabiso's cattle farm in Manamela village. Unlike shop workers who earned cash, farm laborers like Mpho were traditionally paid with livestock - in his case, one cow per year of service.

"My first cow was skinny and old," Mpho remembers. "But to me, she represented my first asset. I cared for her like she was made of gold, and she produced two calves within three years."

In our village, cattle were like walking bank accounts. That first cow I earned was my start-up capital - she became the foundation of everything I have today.

The Cattle Multiplication Strategy

After five years of farm work (earning five cows), Mpho made a strategic decision to stop working for others and focus on growing his own herd:

  • Bred his cows carefully, keeping female calves to expand his herd
  • Traded male calves for farming equipment with neighboring farmers
  • Sold one cow every two years to buy small parcels of land

By age 25, Mpho owned 22 cattle and 15 hectares of land - all from that initial "salary" of five cows.

Transition to Commercial Farming

The real transformation came when Mpho attended an agricultural workshop in Maseru:

2005

Learned about rotational grazing - doubled his herd size in three years

2010

Purchased first tractor by selling 8 cattle

2015

Won government tender to supply school feeding programs

Present

Owns 500 cattle, 3 tractors, and employs 12 farm workers

Modern Farming with Traditional Roots

Today, Mpho's operation blends modern techniques with village wisdom:

Traditional Methods

  • Still uses communal grazing lands for young cattle
  • Barters livestock for services with other villagers
  • Maintains relationships with local cattle herders

Modern Additions

  • Uses solar-powered electric fencing
  • Tracks herd health with mobile app
  • Accepts digital payments for meat sales

Lessons from the Land

Mpho's advice for young villagers:

Value All Assets

Livestock, knowledge, and relationships all have value that can grow.

Convert Wisely

Know when to trade animals for equipment or opportunities.

Never Stop Learning

One workshop changed everything - keep seeking knowledge.
