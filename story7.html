From Goats to Tech Guru

How 6 goats per year built a digital empire

The Herder Who Loved Technology

At age 15, Tsepo began working as a goat herder for a wealthy farmer in Serutle village. His annual payment followed tradition—6 goats per year. But unlike others, Tsepo spent his free time in the nearest internet café, teaching himself to code.

"I traded my first goat for a used laptop," Tsepo recalls. "The farmer laughed, but that laptop changed my life. While others saw goats as food or savings, I saw them as my ticket to the digital world."

In the village, goats are money. I used mine to buy knowledge instead of things. Every goat I sold paid for another online course, another book, another piece of equipment.

The Goat-to-Code Strategy

Tsepo’s unconventional path to tech success:

Year 1:

Traded 2 goats for a refurbished laptop and mobile internet

Year 2:

Sold 3 goats to pay for online coding bootcamp

Year 3:

Used last goat as collateral to rent a small office in Maseru

The First Big Project

Tsepo’s breakthrough came when he developed an app for local farmers:

Livestock Tracker

Digital records for cattle health and sales

Weather Alerts

Localized forecasts for crop planning

Market Prices

Real-time updates on crop and livestock values

The app, Mafisa Digital, now has over 5,000 users across Lesotho.

Building a Tech Hub

Tsepo’s company, Village Tech Solutions, now provides:

Rural Internet

Solar-powered WiFi hotspots

App Development

Custom software for African businesses

Tech Training

Coding classes for village youth
