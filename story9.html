From Cattle to Construction Magnate

How 8 cattle per year built a housing empire

The Herder Who Built Houses

At age 19, Mpho left school to work as a cattle herder, earning 8 cattle per year. While others invested in livestock, Mpho saw a different opportunity. "I noticed our village needed proper houses," he recalls. "I traded my first 3 cattle for carpentry tools and apprenticed with a builder in Maseru."

Those cattle were my construction materials. Each animal I sold bought cement, roofing sheets, and eventually my first truck. I built my business one cow at a time.

The Building Blocks of Success

Mpho's strategic approach:

Phase 1

Skills Development (Years 1-2)

  • Traded 5 cattle for vocational training
  • Mastered bricklaying, plumbing, and electrical work
Phase 2

First Projects (Years 3-5)

  • Built 3 village homes using cattle profits
  • Established reputation for quality work
Phase 3

Expansion (Year 6+)

  • Won government housing contracts
  • Purchased heavy equipment

The Housing Revolution

Mpho's company, Ntlo ea Khomo (House of Cattle), transformed village construction:

Affordable Homes

200+ houses built at 40% below market rate

Modern Amenities

Solar power and running water standard

Local Employment

35 full-time workers from Serutle

Innovating Traditional Building

Mpho blends old and new techniques:

Traditional

  • Mud and thatch construction
  • Community labor parties
  • Natural insulation

Modern

  • Reinforced concrete foundations
  • Prefabricated components
  • Energy-efficient designs

Blueprints for Success

Mpho's advice for young builders:

Master Your Craft

Quality work brings repeat business

Invest in Equipment

Good tools make the job easier

Build Your Team

Train others to multiply capacity
