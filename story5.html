From Sheep to Skyscrapers

How 5 sheep per year built a construction empire

The Shepherd's Apprentice

At age 14, Lebohang began working for a wealthy livestock owner in Serutle village. His annual payment came in the traditional form for boys - 5 sheep per year of shepherding service.

"I would spend weeks in the mountains watching the flocks," Lebohang recalls. "During rainy seasons, I learned to repair the stone kraals. That's when I discovered my passion for building."

Those sheep weren't just animals - they were my construction materials. Each woolly paycheck could be converted into bricks and mortar if you knew how.

The Wool-to-Walls Strategy

By age 18, Lebohang had accumulated 20 sheep through his labor contracts. His conversion strategy:

Step 1: Wool Profits

Sold wool annually to buy basic tools (trowel, level, hammer)

Step 2: Strategic Trading

Traded 8 sheep for an apprenticeship with a Maseru builder

Step 3: First Project

Used remaining sheep as collateral to build his first house

The Building Breakthrough

Lebohang's big opportunity came when he identified three unmet needs:

School Renovations

Village schools needed durable classrooms

Clinic Construction

New health posts required nationwide

Water Infrastructure

Water tanks and pipelines in demand

His company, Basotho Builders, now specializes in these three areas.

Traditional Techniques, Modern Business

Lebohang blends old and new approaches:

Stone Masonry

3D Design Software

Community Labor

Equipment Rentals

Livestock Payments

Digital Invoicing

Construction Wisdom

Lebohang's advice for young builders:

Measure Twice

Planning prevents wasted resources

Build Your Team

Quality workers are your foundation

Reinvest in Tools

Good equipment multiplies output

Basotho Builders Today:

  • 45 full-time employees
  • 12 ongoing projects
  • Still accepts livestock payments
  • Training program for village youth
