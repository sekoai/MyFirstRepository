The Restaurant Owner

From door-to-door meals to a culinary empire

The Village Chef

At 17, Matseliso started cooking extra portions of her family's meals and selling them to neighbors in Serutle village. "I carried pots on my head," she remembers. "The teachers' college paid me 10 maloti per meal. In six months, I saved enough to buy a charcoal stove."

My secret was grandmother's recipes. While others used expensive ingredients, I cooked with moroho greens and cheap cuts. My customers said it tasted like home.

The Expansion Strategy

Matseliso's keys to scaling up:

  1. Consistent Quality: Same recipes across all locations
  2. Staff Training: Taught village women her techniques
  3. Smart Locations: Opened near schools and bus stations
  4. Community Ties: Sources ingredients from local farmers
