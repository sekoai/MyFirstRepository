The Shopkeeper's Journey

From village shop assistant to multiple shop owner in Maseru

Humble Beginnings

At just 15 years old, Thabo started working at the only village shop in Serutle. With no cash payment available, his annual contract was for 9 goats - paid at the end of each year.

"I would wake up at 4am to clean the shop before it opened at 6am," Thabo recalls. "My duties included stocking shelves, serving customers, and keeping accounts in a handwritten book."

Those early years taught me the value of hard work and customer service. Even when paid in goats, I knew I was building something valuable.

The Turning Point

After 5 years of working for goats, Thabo had accumulated a small herd. He made his first business move by selling some goats to buy basic goods which he resold from his family's home.

"I started with just soap, matches, and paraffin - items people needed daily. My mother allowed me to use one room in our house as a tiny shop."

Growth and Expansion

Within two years, Thabo's home shop was making more profit than the village shop where he worked. He made the bold decision to focus on his own business full-time.

Key milestones in Thabo's journey:

  • Year 1: Home shop with basic goods
  • Year 3: Rented a small shop space in the village
  • Year 5: Opened second location in neighboring village
  • Year 8: First shop in Maseru with cash registers
  • Present: 5 shops across Maseru with 15 employees

Lessons Learned

Thabo shares his key lessons for success:

Start Small

Don't wait for perfect conditions. Begin with what you have.

Know Your Customers

Understand what people need and serve them well.

Reinvest Profits

Grow your business by putting money back into it.
